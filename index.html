Lawn Knowledge

Common Grass Types in Memphis

Memphis lawns range from warm-season standbys to cool-season options depending on sun, shade, and homeowner preference.

Bermuda

Bermuda Grass

The go-to for sunny, high-traffic Memphis yards. Durable, heat-loving, and grows thick in summer.

Zoysia

Zoysia Grass

Popular for its dense, uniform look. A strong choice for homeowners who want a lush, finished appearance.

Tall Fescue

Tall Fescue

Great for shadier yards. Stays visually active in cooler stretches and gives a classic lawn look.

St. Augustine

St. Augustine

Broader blade texture with a fuller look. Works well in partial shade across the Memphis area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first visit.

How often should I schedule lawn service in Memphis?
Most residential lawns benefit from weekly or bi-weekly mowing during the growing season (April–October) and monthly maintenance in cooler months. Commercial properties are typically on weekly contracts for consistent curb appeal.
Do you serve both commercial and residential clients?
Yes — all of our services are available to both commercial property owners and residential homeowners. We serve office parks, retail centers, HOAs, apartment communities, and single-family homes throughout the Memphis area.
Do I need to be home during a service visit?
Not at all. Once we have your property details and preferences, our crew handles everything on schedule. You can be at work or away and come home to a beautifully maintained yard.
What is your satisfaction guarantee?
If you're not completely satisfied with any service, we'll return for a free re-visit. No questions asked. We're not done until you're happy with the results.
Do you offer one-time services or only recurring plans?
Both! We offer flexible one-time cleanups, seasonal refreshes, and ongoing recurring plans. Whatever fits your property and schedule, we can work with it.
Do you serve Southaven, MS and surrounding areas?
Yes. We serve the full Memphis metro including Southaven, Olive Branch, Horn Lake, and Hernando in DeSoto County, MS, as well as all Shelby County communities in Tennessee.
